Ten years of product design, mostly in complicated domains — health records, investing, urban transport. The common thread: products people rely on, where the details matter.

I start with research because it's cheaper to learn before building than after. Time with real users usually points to the answer; my job is to ask good questions and keep the design simple.

I've been the only designer in the company and one of many in a large cross-functional team — both suit me. Based in Berlin, open to full-time roles and freelance projects.

The process
01
Listen first

User interviews, diary studies, watching people use the thing. No sketching until there's a clear picture of what's actually going wrong.

02
Make it concrete early

Storyboards and rough wireframes before anything polished. It's easier to change direction while the work still looks unfinished.

03
Test with real people

Prototypes close enough to real to surface honest reactions. Then iterate until it actually works.

04
Stay until it ships

Working closely with engineers through the build. If a design doesn't hold up once it's actually coded, it wasn't done.
